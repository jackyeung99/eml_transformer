Area Forecast Discussion
National Weather Service Indianapolis IN
625 PM EST Sat Jan 4 2025

.KEY MESSAGES...

- Below normal temperatures expected for the foreseeable future. 
Bitterly cold conditions likely by mid week next week.

- WINTER STORM Warning in effect across much of central IN for a 
significant winter storm expected to impact the region Sunday - 
Monday.

- Winter Weather Advisory in effect for northern portions of central 
Indiana where lower snowfall totals and overall impacts are expected.

&&

.SHORT TERM (This evening through Monday)... 
Issued at 322 PM EST Sat Jan 4 2025

OVERVIEW -

1) WINTER STORM WARNING across much of central Indiana for a winter 
storm expected Sunday into Monday, with potential for significant to 
major impacts, in the form of heavy snow, sleet/ice accumulations, 
and gusty winds/blowing and drifting. A WINTER WEATHER ADVISORY is 
in effect for northwestern portions of central Indiana where 
uncertainty remains regarding overall impacts.

2) Bitterly cold conditions arriving in the wake of the winter 
storm, likely persisting well into the week 2 period before 
improvement.

WINTER STORM SUNDAY INTO MONDAY -

The Ohio Valley has an impending winter storm of which will approach 
central Indiana Sunday morning, with impacts through most of Monday. 
This particular set-up is not typical for a large impactful winter 
system, and thus is creating some challenges in regards to the 
forecast. 

Most of the abnormalities lie in the presence of a broad deep trough 
over the eastern portions of Canada and New England. This trough is 
working two fold, both limiting northeastward progression of the 
impending low, and keeping antecedent conditions well below 
freezing. Regardless, a trough will push eastward today and tonight, 
with a resulting low developing generally over the Ozarks/Mid-
Mississippi Valley. 

Due to this Canadian trough, typical phasing of the low will be 
shunted, keeping progression mostly eastward. Latest 12Z guidance 
has much more precision with the low passing through southern KY 
Sunday through Monday, allowing for much more model consistency. The 
central TN solutions have become nearly non-existent, leading to a 
return northward in the snowfall axis to along and just south of the 
I-70 border. Timing continues to be consistent in the latest suite 
of model guidance with precipitation onset occurring from 9AM in far 
SW central Indiana, to early afternoon across the I-70 corridor. 

Precipitation type issues is likely to occur Sunday afternoon 
through the evening and overnight as a 50-70kt 700-850mb jet will 
push much warmer air into central Indiana above a well below 
freezing near surface layer. The current thought is for much greater 
coverage of sleet vs freezing rain for central Indiana due to the 
entire 0-3km layer remaining below freezing. With that said, there 
is some uncertainty due to potential for the warm nose to be warmer 
than 3C for portions of southern central Indiana. If this does 
occur, rain will likely have a hard time re-freezing before reaching 
the surface resulting in more widespread FZRA. Along and north of I-
70, the warm nose should remain below freezing with any 
precipitation falling rimed, dense snow through Sunday afternoon. 

Dynamic cooling as the CCB strengthens will likely push mixed 
precipitation further into southern IN and KY Sunday evening through 
Sunday night. It is at this time that greatest snowfall rates are 
most likely for central Indiana with 700-500mb vertical lift 
increasing and co-located inside the DGZ. One big change is the 
arrival of a mid level dry slot Sunday night. This would lead to 
drying of the DGZ, and a lack of ice crystal growth in the cloud 
cover. This would lead to widespread FZDZ, currently expected south 
of I-70. 

Another round of high SLR snow is expected within deformation early 
Monday morning. This has been better analyzed in the coarser 
grid- space models as the WCB is expected to overcome the Canadian
trough and develop a TROWAL. Given the high SLRs and narrow 
banding potential with this portion of the storm, a wide range in 
6hour snowfall amounts is possible between 09Z and 15Z. 

Snow will begin to taper off as the system departs to the east, dry 
advection aloft begins, and forcing quickly weakens during the day 
on Monday, but light accumulations may continue through Monday 
afternoon. 

*******************************************************************

Summary of Winter Storm: 

Given the strong forcing, plentiful moisture, and long duration of 
the event due to the orientation and motion of the primary 
precipitation band with the system, probabilities are still high for 
warning criteria of 5 inches, especially for areas along and south 
of I70. For this reason a Winter Storm Warming is in effect for a 
majority of central Indiana. NW portions of central Indiana, there 
exhibits greater uncertainty with a sharp northward gradient in 
snowfall totals expected in these areas. Greatest snowfall amounts 
are likely to remain south of the I-70 corridor; current forecast is 
for snowfall totals as high as 12 inches. Areas that see any mixing 
of sleet or freezing rain will likely see some decrease in snowfall 
totals on Sunday, however impacts could still be just as bad due to 
ice accretion. Winds will also be a concern, particularly Sunday 
night into Monday, with blowing and drifting likely becoming at 
least a moderate concern in areas that did not receive freezing 
drizzle Sunday night. Frequent gusts in the 30-35 MPH range and 
locally higher gusts are expected. 

********************************************************************

&&

.LONG TERM (Monday night through Saturday)... 
Issued at 322 PM EST Sat Jan 4 2025

Behind the coming winter storm, the cold airmass will further settle 
in and is expected to persist well into the week 2 period. Another 
round of snow will be possible at the end of next week, but any 
details are uncertain at this time. 

Confidence is high on cold air taking hold throughout the long term, 
especially as surface high pressure moves through. Highs will range 
from the teens to 20s with lows largely in the single digits to 
below zero. The coldest night is expected to be Wednesday night 
which will also see wind chills near -5 to -10.

While it's not really reflected in PoPs at this time due to 
uncertainty, can't rule out some flurries and light snow on Tuesday 
and Wednesday along the western counties and then northern counties 
from a combination of moisture on the backside of the exiting low 
and a swath of Lake Effect enhancement reaching central Indiana. At 
such cold temperatures, it also doesn't take much energy to produce 
flurries. So, could see an increase of PoPs at times for midweek in 
subsequent forecast packages. 

Towards the end of the week and into next weekend, models are in 
agreement of another system or two moving through the eastern U.S. 
potentially bringing more snow to the area and another bout of cold 
air. There is not enough agreement or consistency to have any 
confidence on details at this time.

&&

.AVIATION (00Z TAF Issuance)...
Issued at 624 PM EST Sat Jan 4 2025

Impacts: 

- MVFR ceilings developing around or shortly after daybreak Sunday 
  at KBMG and KHUF then expanding northeast 
- Arrival of snow after 12Z Sunday with rapidly deteriorating 
  conditions into Sunday afternoon

Discussion: 

Quiet conditions will continue through the night with gradually 
lowering high then mid level clouds. Winds will be light.

Clouds will continue to thicken and lower tonight with MVFR stratus 
developing at both KBMG and KHUF around or shortly after daybreak 
Sunday along with light snow. Snow will expand northeast across 
central Indiana into Sunday afternoon with rapidly deteriorating 
conditions as heavier snow lifts into the region at both KBMG and 
KHUF, likely reaching KIND by mid afternoon. Snow will continue into 
the evening hours.

Winds will become northeast on Sunday with an increase in speed 
later in the day.
